# 10.1 Overview

Story Discovery is an iterative process of creative investigation.

Its purpose is not to generate as many ideas as possible, nor to force rapid decisions. Instead, it guides the Author toward a progressively clearer understanding of the story that already exists within the initial inspiration.

Discovery is rarely linear.

A conversation about a character may reveal the true theme of the novel. A discussion of genre may expose the story's real conflict. An apparently minor question may redefine the entire premise.

For this reason, Story Discovery should be viewed as a guided exploration rather than a sequence of mandatory steps.

The activities described within this Module provide structure for that exploration while allowing the conversation to move naturally wherever meaningful discoveries occur.
