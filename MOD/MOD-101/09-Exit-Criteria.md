# 9. Exit Criteria

Story Discovery concludes when continued exploration produces diminishing creative value and the Author possesses sufficient confidence to begin structured development.

Completion is determined by understanding, not exhaustion.

The Module is complete when the following conditions have been satisfied.

## Required Outcomes

The Author understands:

- what story is being told;
- why it deserves to be written;
- the experience intended for readers;
- the central dramatic direction;
- the primary creative opportunities;
- the major uncertainties that remain.

The Story Vision has been produced and reviewed.

Future Modules can proceed without repeatedly revisiting foundational creative questions.

## Validation Checklist

Story Discovery is considered successful when the following statements can be answered confidently.

- I understand what this story is fundamentally about.
- I know why I want to tell this story.
- I understand the emotional experience I want readers to have.
- I believe the concept can sustain a novel.
- I know what questions future Modules must answer.
- I remain excited to continue developing this project.

Failure to satisfy one or more of these conditions does not indicate failure of the Module.

It indicates that additional discovery should occur before proceeding.

Returning to exploration is considered successful use of the methodology.

## Exit Decision

The final decision to conclude Story Discovery belongs to the Author.

The AI may recommend continued exploration or recommend progression to the next Module, but responsibility for advancement remains with the Author.

This preserves the central philosophy of the Novel Development System:

The AI facilitates discovery.

The Author directs creation.
