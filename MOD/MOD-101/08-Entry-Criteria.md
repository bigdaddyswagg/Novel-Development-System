# 8. Entry Criteria

Story Discovery may begin whenever the Author believes a concept is worth investigating.

The Module intentionally has very few prerequisites because creative inspiration rarely arrives in an orderly fashion.

## Required Conditions

The following conditions must exist before beginning Story Discovery:

- The Author possesses a genuine story idea, regardless of its completeness.
- The Author is willing to explore rather than immediately solve the story.
- The Author accepts that uncertainty is a natural part of discovery.
- No downstream design work has been irrevocably committed.

Perfect preparation is not required.

Curiosity is.

## Recommended Conditions

Although not mandatory, Story Discovery benefits from:

- Dedicated creative time.
- A willingness to challenge initial assumptions.
- Freedom to discard weak ideas.
- Openness to unexpected discoveries.
- A collaborative mindset.

## Entry Validation

Before beginning, confirm the following:

- Is there genuine enthusiasm for the idea?
- Does the concept continue to generate curiosity?
- Is the Author willing to investigate rather than defend the first version of the idea?

If the answer to these questions is yes, Story Discovery should begin immediately.
