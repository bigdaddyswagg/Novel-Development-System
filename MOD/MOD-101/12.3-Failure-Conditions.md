# 12.3 Failure Conditions

The following conditions indicate that Story Discovery may no longer be progressing effectively.

## Premature Solutioning

The project moves into outlining, plotting, scene design, or detailed implementation before the story's identity has been established.

### Indicators

- Detailed planning replaces exploration.
- Structural decisions are made to compensate for an unclear premise.
- Questions are answered before they have been fully understood.

### Recovery

Suspend implementation activities and return to exploratory discussion focused on the Story Vision.

## Idea Attachment

The Author becomes committed to an early idea despite evidence that stronger alternatives exist.

### Indicators

- Alternative approaches are dismissed without consideration.
- Discussions repeatedly defend existing decisions rather than investigate new possibilities.
- Curiosity declines while certainty increases.

### Recovery

Temporarily separate the underlying objective from the proposed solution and explore multiple approaches to achieving the same narrative purpose.

## Discovery Drift

Conversation gradually loses focus and no longer contributes to understanding the story.

### Indicators

- Topics become increasingly disconnected.
- Sessions generate large volumes of notes with few meaningful insights.
- Previously identified objectives are no longer being advanced.

### Recovery

Review the current Story Vision, summarize recent discoveries, identify unresolved questions, and re-establish a clear focus for subsequent sessions.

## Concept Exhaustion

The original idea no longer generates creative energy.

### Indicators

- Sessions consistently produce diminishing enthusiasm.
- New ideas feel forced rather than discovered.
- Development continues primarily from a sense of obligation.

### Recovery

Determine whether the issue lies with the implementation or the underlying concept.

If the Story Vision no longer represents the story the Author wishes to write, Story Discovery should evolve accordingly rather than preserving an outdated direction.

## False Completion

The Author believes Story Discovery is complete simply because enough documentation has been produced.

### Indicators

- Confidence is based on document length rather than understanding.
- Fundamental questions remain unresolved.
- Downstream planning repeatedly revisits foundational issues.

### Recovery

Evaluate completion against the validation criteria established in Section 11 rather than against the volume of produced material.
