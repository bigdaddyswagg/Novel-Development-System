# 6. Inputs

Story Discovery accepts inspiration in whatever form it naturally exists.

The Module does not require the Author to organize ideas before beginning. In fact, disorganized inspiration often contains valuable connections that become visible only through exploration.

## Acceptable Inputs

Story Discovery accepts, but is not limited to:

- Story ideas.
- Premises.
- Characters.
- Scenes.
- Dialogue.
- Themes.
- Images.
- Dreams.
- Questions.
- Research notes.
- Historical events.
- Personal experiences.
- Emotional observations.
- Music.
- Artwork.
- Existing manuscripts.
- Voice recordings.
- Brainstorming sessions.
- Conversations.
- "What if?" questions.

No single input is considered superior.

Story Discovery adapts to the Author rather than requiring the Author to adapt to the process.

## Required Input

The only mandatory input is genuine creative interest.

Without authentic curiosity from the Author, meaningful Story Discovery cannot occur.

## Optional Supporting Inputs

The Author may also provide:

- Existing research.
- Genre references.
- Comparable works.
- Character sketches.
- Worldbuilding notes.
- Journals.
- Photographs.
- Maps.
- Mood boards.
- Inspiration collections.

These materials serve the discovery process but do not define it.
