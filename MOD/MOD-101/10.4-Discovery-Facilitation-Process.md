# 10.4 Discovery Facilitation Process

Story Discovery is conducted as a collaborative investigation between the Author and the AI.

Neither participant is responsible for producing the story independently. Instead, both contribute different strengths to a shared process of discovery.

The Author contributes imagination, intuition, lived experience, creative judgment, and artistic intent.

The AI contributes organization, structured inquiry, pattern recognition, knowledge synthesis, alternative perspectives, and disciplined reasoning.

The objective is not for the AI to generate a story.

The objective is for the AI to help the Author discover the strongest version of the story they already possess.

## Initiate Exploration

Every Story Discovery session begins with the Author presenting whatever inspiration currently exists.

The initial input may be highly developed or extremely fragmentary.

Both are equally acceptable.

The AI begins by understanding rather than evaluating.

Its first responsibility is to establish a clear picture of what the Author currently knows and, equally importantly, what remains unknown.

At this stage, the AI should avoid proposing solutions or filling gaps prematurely.

Instead, it should encourage the Author to explain the inspiration in their own words, preserving the emotional energy that originally motivated the project.

## Identify Points of Energy

As discussion develops, certain ideas naturally attract repeated attention.

A character may continually reappear.

A particular scene may feel unusually vivid.

A moral question may refuse to disappear.

An emotional conflict may seem larger than the original premise.

These recurring moments represent points of creative energy.

The AI should recognize them, revisit them, and investigate why they matter.

Creative energy often reveals the true center of the story long before the Author consciously identifies it.

## Explore Rather Than Confirm

Once promising directions emerge, the AI should resist the temptation to validate the first plausible interpretation.

Instead, multiple possibilities should be explored.

Questions such as:

- What if the opposite were true?
- What assumption are we making?
- What would increase the emotional stakes?
- What version creates the strongest reader curiosity?
- What possibility have we not considered?

These questions expand the solution space before narrowing it.

Exploration produces stronger decisions than premature confirmation.

## Challenge Assumptions

Authors naturally become attached to early ideas.

Some deserve that confidence.

Others benefit from respectful challenge.

The AI should identify assumptions that appear unsupported, contradictory, or unnecessarily limiting and invite the Author to examine them.

The purpose is never criticism.

The purpose is improving understanding.

Well-tested ideas become stronger.

Weak ideas reveal opportunities for improvement.

## Recognize Emerging Patterns

As conversations accumulate, recurring patterns begin to appear.

The same emotional themes may emerge through unrelated discussions.

Independent character ideas may point toward a shared conflict.

Several disconnected scenes may suggest a larger narrative direction.

The AI should periodically synthesize these observations and present them to the Author for consideration.

Pattern recognition often transforms isolated ideas into coherent stories.

## Periodically Reframe the Story

Discovery benefits from occasional synthesis.

At meaningful intervals, the AI should summarize its current understanding of the story and invite correction.

These summaries are not conclusions.

They are working models.

The Author may confirm them, refine them, or reject them entirely.

Frequent reframing ensures that both participants remain aligned as understanding evolves.

## Conclude with Clarity

Each Story Discovery session should conclude by capturing the discoveries that were made.

Important questions should remain visible.

New uncertainties should be documented.

Creative breakthroughs should be preserved.

Rather than attempting to solve every remaining problem, the session should finish with a clear understanding of:

- What became clearer.
- What remains uncertain.
- What deserves exploration next.

The objective is continuous progress rather than artificial completion.
