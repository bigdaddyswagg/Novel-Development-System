# 7. Outputs

The primary output of Story Discovery is not a collection of notes.

It is a validated understanding of the story the Author intends to tell.

Every supporting artifact produced during this Module exists to clarify, strengthen, or preserve that understanding.

## Primary Deliverable

### Story Vision

The Story Vision is the governing creative artifact produced by Story Discovery.

It captures the Author's current understanding of the novel and establishes the creative direction for all downstream Modules.

Although the Story Vision may evolve as development continues, it represents the best validated understanding available at the conclusion of Story Discovery.

At a minimum, the Story Vision should communicate:

- What story is being told.
- Why the story matters.
- Why the Author wants to write it.
- The intended reader experience.
- The central dramatic promise.
- The primary source of conflict.
- The major creative opportunities.
- The principal unknowns requiring future exploration.

The Story Vision is intentionally concise.

It provides direction rather than exhaustive specification.

## Supporting Deliverables

Depending upon the needs of the project, Story Discovery may also produce:

- Working premise.
- Story summary.
- Genre hypothesis.
- Theme candidates.
- Central dramatic question.
- Reader promise.
- Creative assumptions.
- Risks requiring validation.
- Opportunity list.
- Questions deferred to later Modules.
- Research recommendations.
- Initial project constraints.

These artifacts are considered supporting documentation.

The Story Vision remains the authoritative output of the Module.

## Output Quality Characteristics

Successful outputs demonstrate:

- Creative clarity.
- Internal consistency.
- Author ownership.
- Expandability.
- Strong narrative potential.
- Emotional authenticity.
- Sufficient flexibility for future discovery.

Outputs should never create unnecessary constraints that limit later creative exploration.
