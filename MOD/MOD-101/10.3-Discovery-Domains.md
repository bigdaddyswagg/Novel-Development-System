# 10.3 Discovery Domains

Although every project follows a unique path, Story Discovery generally explores several recurring domains.

These domains are not phases.

They are lenses through which the emerging story can be examined.

A single conversation may move naturally between several domains.

## Story Premise

The initial premise establishes the starting point for discovery.

Discussion focuses on:

- What initially inspired the story.
- Why the concept feels compelling.
- What makes the premise different from familiar stories.
- What possibilities appear immediately obvious.
- What possibilities remain unexplored.

The objective is not to perfect the premise.

It is to understand its potential.

## Conflict

Conflict gives the story direction.

Discovery explores questions such as:

- What creates tension?
- What cannot continue unchanged?
- What difficult choices will eventually become necessary?
- What forces oppose one another?
- Why is the conflict emotionally meaningful?

Conflict should emerge naturally from the story rather than being artificially imposed.

## Characters

Characters are explored as sources of dramatic possibility rather than complete biographies.

Initial discussion may examine:

- Motivation.
- Fear.
- Desire.
- Internal contradiction.
- Relationships.
- Transformation potential.

Detailed character development belongs to later Modules.

The objective here is discovering who the story appears to be about.

## Theme

Themes are treated as discoveries rather than assignments.

Story Discovery explores recurring questions, values, beliefs, and emotional truths that naturally emerge from discussion.

The AI should avoid forcing thematic conclusions before the story reveals them.

## Reader Experience

Every novel creates an experience.

Story Discovery investigates:

- What readers should feel.
- What questions should keep them turning pages.
- What promises the story makes.
- What emotional journey is being offered.

Understanding the intended experience often clarifies many later creative decisions.

## Genre

Genre is explored as a set of reader expectations rather than a marketing label.

The goal is understanding:

- What promises readers expect.
- What emotional experience the story intends to deliver.
- What conventions may strengthen the work.
- Where innovation is appropriate.

Genre should support the story.

The story should never be distorted merely to satisfy genre expectations.
