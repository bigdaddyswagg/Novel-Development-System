# 4. Scope

Story Discovery defines the creative foundation upon which the entire Novel Development System is built.

Its scope is intentionally limited to discovering the story before attempting to engineer it.

The Module focuses on understanding the story's identity, purpose, and potential rather than producing detailed implementation artifacts. Throughout Story Discovery, the Author and AI collaborate to explore possibilities, challenge assumptions, identify opportunities, and gradually reveal the story's underlying intent.

This Module deliberately emphasizes discovery over decision making. Premature certainty is considered a greater risk than temporary ambiguity.

## Included Within Scope

Story Discovery includes activities that help the Author understand what story is emerging and why it deserves to be written.

These activities include, but are not limited to:

- Exploring the initial story premise.
- Identifying the central source of dramatic interest.
- Discovering the emotional core of the story.
- Clarifying the Author's creative intentions.
- Examining multiple possibilities before selecting a direction.
- Identifying likely genres and reader expectations.
- Exploring major themes and questions.
- Testing the novelty and sustainability of the concept.
- Identifying creative risks, assumptions, and unknowns.
- Evaluating whether the concept possesses sufficient depth for a full-length novel.
- Producing a validated Story Vision suitable for downstream development.

Throughout these activities, exploration remains iterative. New discoveries may require revisiting earlier assumptions, refining ideas, or discarding previously favored concepts in favor of stronger alternatives.

This iterative behavior is expected and encouraged.

## Outside the Scope

Story Discovery intentionally avoids activities that depend upon an already validated Story Vision.

These include:

- Detailed outlining.
- Scene design.
- Chapter organization.
- Character biographies.
- Worldbuilding beyond conceptual exploration.
- Timeline construction.
- Plot architecture.
- Beat sheets.
- Scene sequencing.
- Manuscript drafting.
- Revision planning.
- Publishing strategy.

These activities belong to later Modules where they can be performed with the benefit of a well-understood creative foundation.

Attempting them during Story Discovery frequently leads to unnecessary rework as the story continues to evolve.

## Guiding Principle

The purpose of Story Discovery is not to answer every question.

Its purpose is to discover the right questions.

Once those questions are understood, the remaining Modules can answer them systematically.
